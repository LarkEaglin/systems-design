Project-Based Recreation Development Training

This initiative is not just a staff training. It is the foundation of a recreation system. The intention is to create a consistent, replicable approach to how programs are designed and facilitated across recreation centers.

Overview

This professional development training introduces a structured approach to recreation programming as a system, rather than a collection of isolated activities. Designed for recreation leaders, the training equips staff with the tools to intentionally design programs that produce meaningful participant outcomes.Using an experiential, project-based model, participants actively build a program during the training, applying a standardized framework that can be replicated across sites. By the end of the session, each participant leaves with a practical program blueprint and a clear understanding of how to deliver high-quality, consistent programming within their recreation center.

Organizational Outcomes

  • Standardize programming quality across all sites
  • Empower recreation leaders with clear tools and structure
  • Improve youth engagement through intentional program design
  • Make program planning more efficient, consistent, and scalable

Core Learning Themes

  • Curriculum-Based Program Development: Staff are introduced to a 6–8 week programming framework that supports skill progression, consistency, and measurable growth.
  • Experience Design & Engagement Participants explore how to structure program flow and maintain engagement to create meaningful participant experiences through intentional sequencing.
  • Recreation Programming as System Design Participants learn to approach programming as a structured process with defined outcomes—shifting from activity planning to intentional program architecture.

Algorithmic Thinking

This training applies principles of algorithmic thinking to program design, helping staff translate complex ideas into clear, repeatable steps. Participants practice decomposition by breaking programs into core components such as outcomes, sessions, activities, materials, and staffing. Through abstraction, they focus on essential elements—goals, audience, and structure—so programs can be adapted and scaled across different contexts.
