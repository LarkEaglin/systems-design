Coloring Connections

A systems-based visual learning series introducing foundational scientific concepts through pattern, play, and observation. Currently distributed across six recreation centers throughout Baltimore City.

Overview

Coloring Connections is a series of interactive coloring books designed to introduce children to foundational scientific concepts through visual exploration and pattern recognition. Each volume connects two core ideas—such as Water & Waves or Waves & Time—guiding learners to recognize relationships across natural systems. Using diagrams, traceable patterns and low-text prompts, the series encourages self-directed discovery. The project emphasizes accessibility and tactile learning, offering an alternative to screen-based education while supporting early systems thinking.

Thinking Design and Pedagogy

Coloring Connections is built on a systems-based approach to learning that prioritizes pattern recognition, visual reasoning and exploratory play over memorization. Rather than presenting information linearly, the books are designed as environments for exploration, where coloring, tracing, and searching become tools for thinking. Each book is intentionally structured to move from concrete observation to more abstract understanding, allowing learners to build intuition through interaction.

Algorithmic Thinking

Coloring Connections integrates algorithmic thinking through interactive page activities that translate complex scientific ideas into repeatable visual processes. Rather than coding on a screen, learners engage with structured games that build foundational thinking skills through play.

book includes a consistent set of activity types—such as mazes, pattern extensions, symmetry drawing, and connect-the-dots—that reinforce key cognitive functions:

  • Pattern recognition — identifying repetition in rhythms, cycles, and natural forms
  • Sequencing — connecting ordered steps through dot-to-dot structures and flow-based diagrams
  • Spatial reasoning — navigating mazes, symmetry exercises and directional systems
  • Decomposition — breaking down systems into smaller visual steps (e.g., tracing wave patterns)
