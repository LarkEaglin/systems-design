Girls in Space: Light the Path

A mission-based STEM camp combining wearable tech and narrative design to introduce youth to creative electronics and problem-solving. Shoe Body Kit: STEMulating Art by Sabrina Thompson.

Overview

Girls in Space: Light the Path is a five-day immersive STEM experience where participants design and construct illuminated “Space Shoes” using textile engineering, conductive thread sewing and Lilypad electronics. The construction of the shoe begins with the STEMulating Art Shoe Kit created by Sabrina Thompson (Girl In Space Club), which serves as the foundation for the design process. Building on this framework, the program introduces soft circuitry and a mission-based narrative environment — the Orbital Innovation Station — where participants take on the role of Space Gear Engineers designing footwear for survival on extreme planetary environments. Through this experience, participants explore circuits and design thinking while developing confidence as creators and innovators.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the fundamentals of soft circuits and wearable electronics
  • Apply design thinking principles to prototype solutions for environmental challenges.
  • Strengthen collaboration and communication through shared problem-solving and peer feedback.
  • Express identity and creativity through personalized design and storytelling
  • Apply design thinking principles to prototype solutions for environmental challenges.

Core Learning Themes

  • Wearable Technology: Participants explore how electronics can be embedded into textiles
  • Systems Integration: Using storytelling and design to guide technical choices.
  • Failure Resilience: Troubleshooting and rebuilding circuits helps normalize failure as part of the engineering process.

Algorithmic Thinking

Algorithmic thinking is introduced through circuit construction and system design. Participants practice sequencing as they plan the flow of electrical current through conductive pathways. They engage in decomposition by breaking down the shoe into functional systems—structure, circuitry and design elements—each requiring specific steps to complete. They also practice abstraction by representing electrical pathways visually through sketches and diagrams before implementing them physically. By translating electrical systems into clear step-by-step processes, participants begin to understand how complex systems are built.
