Algorithmic Thinking

A support doc teaching Problem-Solving Through Systems, Patterns and Play

Overview

The Algorithmic Thinking Lab is a learning design framework that introduces computational thinking through structured problem-solving, pattern recognition and interactive activities. Rather than focusing immediately on coding, the training develops the underlying cognitive skills that make programming possible: breaking problems into parts, recognizing patterns, simplifying complex ideas and building step-by-step solutions. Through games, visual exercises, collaborative challenges and movement-based activities, learners explore how algorithms operate in everyday thinking. This approach helps students build confidence in logical reasoning while developing transferable skills.

Instructional Strategies

  • Hands-On Challenges: Students solve puzzles, build sequences and complete logical tasks.
  • Visual Systems Exploration: Diagrams, flowcharts and visual patterns help learners see relationships between steps and outcomes.
  • Movement-Based Logic Games: Physical activities model programming logic and decision trees in real time
